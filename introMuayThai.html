Introduction of Muay Thai

Introduction
Muay Thai is a national sport in Thailand. People knew it in different names such as Thai boxing. Muay Thai is also called “Art of 8 limbs” which means that it is a discipline that uses knees, elbows, fists, and shins, alongside clinching and sweeping techniques. Therefore, it is different from those classic boxings and karate. In nowadays, Muay Thai is not only in Thailand, but also all over the world.
Muay Thai History
The origin of Muay Thai is still controversial. Some researchers believe that it can be traced to Sukhothai dynasty of Thailand around 13th century. The soldiers need to know how to fight with armed and unarmed. Over time, the unarmed combat skill gradually evolved into the morden Muay Thai.
Modern Muay Thai
Nowadays, Muay Thai has become a combat sport all over the world. This sport includes the elements of boxing. The fighter needs to wear boxing gloves during the match, and 3 to 5 rounds are needed to win the competition. The winner will gain a ring.
